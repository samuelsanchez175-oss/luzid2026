Micro-Drama 1

Paradox

The Loop — TMZ Article Causal Loop

A TMZ article foreshadows her whole story in one moment — the taxi driver who captures her, the desert where they find her. She uses those details to break the loop. It never shows where she dies.

Scene 1 — The Article

Shelby at a coffee shop. She's reading TMZ. The article foreshadows her whole story — in one moment it tells her what's coming. The taxi driver who captures her. Where they find her at the end: a desert. A rich man. A pool. Empty desert background. The article doesn't show where she dies. It just gives her the details. She looks up. She has to break the loop.

Scene 2 — The First Capturer

The man behind her in the coffee shop. One of the images from the TMZ post. She's piecing it together. The taxi driver is next. She has to avoid the sequence — use what she's learned to break the loop before it happens.

Scene 3 — The Taxi

She gets in a taxi. The driver. The article said he captures her. She's inside the prophecy. She uses the details from TMZ — the desert, the pool, the rich man — to change the outcome. Break the loop.

Paradox

Scene 4 — The Loop

She realizes: the TMZ details are a map. The taxi driver. The desert. The pool. The rich man. She has to alter the script. Change the outcome before it happens. The article encourages her — gives her everything she needs to break the loop.

Scene 5 — The Break

She does something different. Uses the foreshadowing against itself. The loop fractures. The prophecy fails. Or does it?

Scene 6 — Resolution

Desert. Empty. A pool. A rich man asks her to dance. The background is just sand — nothing else. We don't see where she dies. We don't see if she broke the loop. We just see the foreshadowed moment. Or the moment she avoided. Cut to black.

Micro-Drama 2

The Stand-In

The Stand-In — Pampered Artist, Rushed Morning, Wrong Girl on Set

Shelby wakes up late. She rushes to the album cover shoot — and discovers someone is already standing in her place. When she tries to reclaim it, security removes her. She goes home and watches her own life play out without her.

Scene 1 — Late Morning

Shelby Carter — pampered, relaxed artist — wakes up late. She's overslept. The shoot is today. She's rushing out the door, pulling on her impractical dress, half-dressed, half-made-up. The chaos of a star who runs on her own time.

Scene 2 — The Rush

She's in an Uber, still adjusting her outfit, checking her phone. She arrives at the lot. Makes it past security — barely. She's late but she's here. The shoot has already started.

Scene 3 — The Replacement

She sees someone standing in her place. On set. In her light. She approaches. Lifts the veil — or moves past the crew — and sees: a woman who looks exactly like her. In that moment, security and production descend. They whisk her away. "Move. Get off the stage." They think she's the impostor. The stand-in has already convinced them. Shelby is escorted out.

The Stand-In

Scene 4 — Exiled

She goes home. The stand-in has taken her place completely. The shoot continues without her. Her album. Her cover. Someone else is living it.

Scene 5 — The Updates

She keeps hearing updates about herself. Social feeds. Texts from her team. "You killed it today." "The shots are incredible." Like White Chicks — the real person at home, sidelined, while the impostor is out there living their life. Shelby watches her own existence unfold from the outside. Cut to black.

Micro-Drama 3

Blood Diamonds

Luxury Crime Micro-Drama — 5 Episodes, 60 sec each

Starring Shelby Carter. Beauty mogul. Socialite. Secret heir to a criminal empire. Designed for unreleased soundtrack — visual beats, minimal dialogue, maximum impact.

Episode 1 — The Night He Died

Red light floods the frame. Close-up: Shelby Carter. Glossy tears. Diamond rings catch the light. Police sirens pulse in the distance. DETECTIVE (off): "Where were you at 10:47?" She doesn't blink. SHELBY (whisper): "Crying." Flashback: marble floor. Blood. Shattered glass. Her face — cold, composed. SHELBY: "He wasn't the target." — Beat. Cut to black. [Track drop.]

Episode 2 — The Empire

Boardroom. Men in suits. She enters — sunglasses, power walk. Removes them. The room goes silent. SHELBY: "It was always mine." She takes the head of the table. No one moves. SHELBY: "I understand the laundering. The offshore accounts. The charity fronts." A smile. Cold. "My father built a beauty empire. He just cleaned more than money." — [Verse builds. She owns the frame.]

Episode 3 — The Betrayal

Security footage plays. Her fiancé. Another woman. Money moving. Shelby watches — expressionless. Luca beside her. LUCA: "He was moving assets behind your back." She wipes a tear. Doesn't break. SHELBY: "He was sleeping in my bed." Beat. SHELBY: "He just miscalculated." She turns off the screen. The room goes dark. — [Bridge. Vulnerability before the turn.]

Blood Diamonds

Episode 4 — Confession in Red

Detective. Interrogation room. Red light. DETECTIVE: "Your gun was used." Close-up: Shelby's hands. Diamond rings. She leans in. SHELBY: "Diamonds are formed under pressure." Lock eyes. "So are confessions." She stands. "No more questions." She walks out. The door closes. — [Chorus. She's in control.]

Episode 5 — The Real Target

Penthouse. Night. Red light. Shelby alone at the window. City below. Luca enters. LUCA: "It's handled." She doesn't turn. LUCA: "The board agreed." She faces him. SHELBY: "He thought he could steal from me." LUCA: "He thought you loved him." Long pause. SHELBY: "I did. Until he tried to own me." She turns back to the window. The city glows. Fade to black. — [Outro. Final beat.]

Micro-Drama 4

Does She Even Work?

The Pool — 5 Episodes

Pool. Gossip eruption. Harsh voices — "she doesn't even work," "what does she do for a living?" A mysterious package arrives. Invitation to an exclusive LA art gala. She proves them wrong.

Episode 1 — Gossip Eruption

She's in a luxurious pool, lounging under the sun, hungover. Half-empty mimosa beside her. In the background: harsh gossip — neighbors, onlookers, overlapping voices. VOICE: "She doesn't even work." "What the hell does she do for a living?" "She's lazy as fuck." "I heard she's a cunt." She's in a floaty, enjoying her mimosa, finishing her glass. Unbothered. Doorbell rings. A delivery arrives at her gate — mysterious package, no return address. Cut to black.

Episode 2 — The Mysterious Package

She opens the package. Inside: a letter and expensive invitations to an exclusive art gala in LA. While others assume she's lazy, she's happy. She opens the invitation, reads it, smiles. She opens her closet — the gala dress for the night is already there. She's going. Cut to black.

Episode 3 — The Gala

Night. She arrives at the gala in the dress. Glamorous, confident. The same people who gossip don't know she's on the list. She belongs. She's not lazy — she's in a world they can't see. Cut to black.

Does She Even Work?

Episode 4 — The Proof

At the gala: she's recognized, greeted, photographed. Maybe she's an artist, a collector, or connected in a way the gossipers don't understand. The invitation wasn't random — she earned it. The haters were wrong. Cut to black.

Episode 5 — The Return

Next day. Back at the pool. Mimosa. Floaty. Same setup as Episode 1 — but she's different. She knows who she is. The gossip is just noise. She doesn't need to prove anything. Fade to black.
