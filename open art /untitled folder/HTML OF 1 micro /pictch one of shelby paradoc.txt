<h1>THE DOUBLE</h1>
<h2>Shelby Carter Micro‑Drama #2</h2>

<p style="margin-top:40px">
Produced by Luzid Productions
</p>

</div>

<div class="page">

<div class="section-title">Logline</div>

<p>
After arriving late to her own album cover shoot, artist Shelby Carter
walks onto set only to discover a woman identical to her already
performing the role. As cameras flash and production continues without
question, Shelby realizes she may have already been replaced.
</p>

</div>

<div class="page">

<div class="section-title">Concept</div>

<p>
The story explores identity, fame, and disposability in celebrity culture.
Shelby’s lateness and growing detachment from her responsibilities create
space for a perfect replacement to step into her life.
</p>

<p>
What begins as confusion on a fashion set evolves into psychological horror
as Shelby watches another version of herself seamlessly inherit her career,
her image, and eventually her entire public identity.
</p>

</div>

<div class="page">

<div class="section-title">Visual Tone</div>

<p>
The visual style mixes fashion photography aesthetics with psychological
thriller tension. Clean studio lighting, stark blue backdrops, and intense
camera flashes contrast with Shelby’s emotional disorientation.
</p>

<p>
Influences include high‑fashion editorials, backstage chaos, and the eerie
stillness of someone watching their own life being lived by someone else.
</p>

</div>
