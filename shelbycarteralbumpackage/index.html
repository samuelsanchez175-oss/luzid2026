How It Ties Back — 7 —

Micro-Drama Treatments

How It Ties Back to the Album Cover

Both micro-dramas connect directly to the album cover imagery. Get A Job — the taxi ride in full glam, the gown flowing — echoes the vintage car aesthetic from the alternate cover (sage & fur, cinematic noir). Same world: luxury, motion, the artist in transit. The Stand-In is set at the album cover shoot itself; the blue room, the unveiling, the moment someone else steps into her frame. The cover becomes the stage. Together, the two worlds (car/ride and studio/shoot) bookend the visual language of the rollout.

Micro-Drama 2

The Stand-In

The Stand-In — Pampered Artist, Rushed Morning, Wrong Girl on Set

Shelby wakes up late. She rushes to the album cover shoot — and discovers someone is already standing in her place. When she tries to reclaim it, security removes her. She goes home and watches her own life play out without her.

Scene 1 — Late Morning

Shelby Carter — pampered, relaxed artist — wakes up late. She's overslept. The shoot is today. She's rushing out the door, pulling on her impractical dress, half-dressed, half-made-up. The chaos of a star who runs on her own time.

Scene 2 — The Rush

She's in an Uber, still adjusting her outfit, checking her phone. She arrives at the lot. Makes it past security — barely. She's late but she's here. The shoot has already started.

Scene 3 — The Replacement

She sees someone standing in her place. On set. In her light. She approaches. Lifts the veil — or moves past the crew — and sees: a woman who looks exactly like her. In that moment, security and production descend. They whisk her away. "Move. Get off the stage." They think she's the impostor. The stand-in has already convinced them. Shelby is escorted out.

— 14 —
The Stand-In (continued) — 15 —

The Stand-In

Scene 4 — Exiled

She goes home. The stand-in has taken her place completely. The shoot continues without her. Her album. Her cover. Someone else is living it.

Scene 5 — The Updates

She keeps hearing updates about herself. Social feeds. Texts from her team. "You killed it today." "The shots are incredible." Like White Chicks — the real person at home, sidelined, while the impostor is out there living their life. Shelby watches her own existence unfold from the outside. Cut to black.
